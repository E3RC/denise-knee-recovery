## Hospital packet notes

These notes were pulled from the uploaded `Ortho Program.pdf` and `Infection Prevention.pdf` so we do not have to keep reopening the scans.

### Ortho program highlights

- Nutrition before and after surgery matters: prioritize protein, hydration, fruits and vegetables, and balanced meals.
- After surgery, try to eat with pain medication, use smaller frequent meals if nausea shows up, and sip fluids instead of gulping them.
- Constipation prevention guidance from the packet:
  - drink at least 6 to 8 cups of fluids daily
  - gradually increase fiber
  - move as soon as cleared
  - prune juice may help
- Day-of-surgery packet note:
  - an adult driver must be available
  - expect IV start, vital sign checks, site marking, and anesthesia review
  - after surgery, expect recovery-room checks, numbness at first, and an early assisted walk with the walker
- Discharge and home recovery guidance:
  - someone should stay with her all day and all night for at least 48 to 72 hours after discharge
  - sit up for meals and during the day
  - do not sit longer than 45 to 60 minutes without standing and stretching
  - walk every hour while awake
  - short frequent walks are better than long walks
  - perform exercises daily and ankle pumps multiple times each hour
  - ice and elevate the surgical leg as much as possible
- Showering and incision care:
  - follow surgeon guidance on when showering is allowed
  - do not soak the incision
  - use a shower chair if needed
  - do not apply creams, lotions, oils, or antibiotic ointments unless instructed
  - redness that spreads, thick yellow drainage, or fever over 101 should be reported
- Eight-week restrictions noted in the packet:
  - no heavy lifting
  - no strenuous yard work
  - no jumping, jerking, pulling, twisting, or running unless approved
- Return-to-work and follow-up notes from the packet:
  - desk work may be possible in 2 to 3 weeks
  - more physical jobs may require 6 to 12 weeks off
  - surgeon follow-up is usually 2 to 3 weeks after surgery
- Call-the-surgeon triggers listed in the packet:
  - pain not controlled with ice, elevation, rest, and medication
  - incision drainage, odor, or wound opening
  - bright red blood from the incision
  - excessive calf or groin pain
  - fever above 101
  - excessive redness around the incision
  - increased numbness or tingling to the surgical leg

### Infection prevention highlights

- Before surgery:
  - no shaving of legs or surgical-area skin for 3 days before surgery
  - shower with CHG antiseptic soap starting 2 days before surgery, then again the night before and the morning of surgery
  - sleep in clean sheets
  - do not wear lotion or perfume
  - tell the surgeon about illness, sore throat, or infection symptoms
  - keep blood sugar under control
  - do not smoke
- During the hospital visit:
  - wash hands before and after eating and after using the bathroom
  - do not touch the incision or bandage
  - ask visitors to wash hands and stay away if sick
  - move feet or walk as allowed to keep blood moving
  - cough and breathe deeply to help prevent pneumonia
- When home:
  - wash hands before and after meals, bathroom use, and touching the incision
  - ask family to wash hands before touching the incision or bandage
  - keep pets away from the incision and do not sleep with pets
  - call the surgeon if the incision is red, warm, has pus, or if fever or chills show up

### CHG shower steps from the infection-prevention sheet

- Use regular shampoo and regular soap first for hair, face, and genitals.
- Turn off the water.
- Apply CHG soap from the neck down with a washcloth.
- Pay special attention to the surgical area.
- Do not use CHG on the face or genitals.
- Leave the soap on the skin for 5 minutes.
- Rinse fully, dry with a freshly washed towel, and put on freshly washed clothes.
- Do not use regular soap after the CHG wash.
